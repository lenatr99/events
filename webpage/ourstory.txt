Our Story - From Conferences to Cats 🐾
Kat and Gil were originally meant to be introduced at Matea’s wedding, but Kat came down with COVID and couldn’t attend. Luckily, a few months later, they all ended up at the same neuroscience conference in San Diego.



Gil was immediately interested, but Kat was completely oblivious. What she did notice was that his Facebook profile had shown him with long hair, and now he had it cut short. She even pointed this out to him, jokingly describing it as false advertising. She also thought he looked stylish, especially with his reflective sunglasses — though little did she know this was not how he usually dressed. She was also impressed (and amused) by how much free conference swag he managed to collect compared to her.



Despite Kat being under the weather again — this time with a bad cold and almost no voice — they spent much of their free time together with Matea and her husband Daniel. One evening as they walked to dinner, Gil asked Kat what the highlight of her day had been. She replied, without hesitation (thinking it would be funny), that she had seen a dog wearing little boots. Gil also pointed out the planets in the night sky during that walk, a small but memorable moment that stayed with them both. On the last night, after some encouragement from Matea over wine, Kat finally messaged him — jokingly asking, “Hi, do you want to get married?” To her surprise, Gil said yes. From that moment, they never stopped talking, not a single day.



Still, with an ocean between them, they quickly had to find out if this connection was real. Kat was in the UK finishing her PhD and Gil was in Houston working on his PhD at Baylor. Kat flew to Houston that January, just two months after the conference, and knew then that she wanted to marry him. During a small birthday celebration at Matea’s apartment complex, Gil even agreed to go swimming with her in the heated pool — in borrowed swimsuits from Matea and Daniel, no less. Kat had been excited to swim in January, something possible thanks to how pools seemed to be everywhere in Houston, and Gil gamely agreed to race her in the water (she won). He was absolutely freezing when they got out, but he was willing to do anything for her.



After a year of visits and long-distance, Kat finally moved to Houston and the two settled into life together — both neuroscientists at Baylor. Along the way they adopted three cats — Elvi, who Kat brought from the UK, Komi, and Lars, who Gil first met on a late-night Walgreens bike ride when the cat screamed at him from the bushes. Lars bonded instantly with Gil, and still loves nothing more than pressing his face against Gil’s beard. The cats have become a huge part of their life together, inspiring songs (including Smelly Cat and weeks of Little Mermaid duets), endless stories, and daily joy.



Their life together is full of shared routines and quirks — like playing Mario Party (where Gil swears he just lets Kat win), trying new restaurants (Gil loves finding food Kat enjoys), and talking about the cats. Gil is known for singing everywhere, while Kat is known for laughing at (and with) him.



The proposal came after a family gathering where Kat had the chance to meet Gil’s relatives and see just how much love and closeness surrounded them. Watching his grandfather at the centre of it all, caring so deeply for every family member, made Kat feel immediately welcomed and part of something special. Around that time, Gil had been quietly building up the courage to propose.



When the moment finally came, he looked at Kat lovingly, a bit teary, clearly trying to ask the question but unable to get the words out. Kat, realizing what he was trying to say, nodded and said yes to ease his nerves before he could finish. He then asked again, this time with words, and once more on one knee, using his late grandmother’s ring as a temporary but deeply meaningful engagement ring.



They’ve shared many milestones since — including Kat’s PhD defense, when Gil and his parents conspired with Kat’s mom to plan a surprise party that brought her all the way from Germany. But the heart of their story is simple: from a missed wedding introduction to a conference full of planets, dogs in boots, and reflective sunglasses, Kat and Gil found each other. And after countless conversations, cross-Atlantic visits, and three cats later, they’re ready to begin their next chapter as husband and wife.
