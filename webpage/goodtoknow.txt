Punctuality

Germans take timekeeping seriously, so trains, tours, and events usually start right on time. For the wedding and any booked activities, it’s a good idea to arrive a few minutes early rather than “fashionably late.”



Money and payments

Germany uses the euro, and most places accept major debit, credit cards, and Apple/Google Pay. That said, small bakeries, cafés, and rural spots may be cash‑only or have a minimum amount, so it’s handy to keep a bit of cash on you. ATMs are easy to find in towns and cities.



Tipping

Tipping is appreciated but more low‑key than in the US. We typically just round up the bill to a convenient amount. You normally tell the server the total you want to pay, including tip, rather than leaving money on the table.



Quiet and public spaces

Germans generally speak more quietly in public than many Americans. On trains and in restaurants, people tend to keep their voices low and avoid loud phone calls. It’s perfectly fine to chat and laugh, just be mindful of the overall volume.



Greetings and politeness

A simple “Hallo” or “Guten Tag” goes a long way when entering a (small) shop or restaurant. Saying “Bitte” (please) and “Danke” (thank you) is very appreciated, and you’ll often make someone smile by trying even a tiny bit of German.



Sundays and opening hours

Shops are closed on Sundays, and smaller towns can feel very quiet. Cafés, restaurants, and bakeries are often open, but it’s smart to plan any shopping (groceries, pharmacies, clothing, etc.) for other days.



Drinking water and bathrooms

Tap water in Germany is safe to drink, but in restaurants they typically serve bottled water, which you pay for. Public bathrooms are clean but sometimes cost a small fee; it’s useful to carry a few coins just in case.



Getting around

Public transport is generally reliable, and people queue in an orderly way and let others off before getting on. When walking, try to keep to the right on sidewalks and escalators so faster people can pass on the left. That being said, the "Deutsche Bahn" has a reputation for train delays.
